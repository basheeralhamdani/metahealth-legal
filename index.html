Privacy Policy

Lantern Charting — operated by Meta Health LLC

Effective date: June 13, 2026 · Last updated: June 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Meta Health LLC (“Meta Health,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects information in connection with the Lantern Charting mobile application (the “App”). Lantern Charting is a clinical documentation tool intended solely for use by authorized healthcare professionals (such as nurses, schedulers, quality-assurance staff, and administrators) who are members of organizations that have contracted with Meta Health. The App is not directed to, or intended for use by, members of the general public or patients.

Important: The App processes Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of healthcare provider organizations (our “Covered Entity” customers). We act as a Business Associate under the U.S. Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and process PHI in accordance with our Business Associate Agreements with those organizations.

1. Who Can Use the App

Access to the App requires an authorized account issued by a healthcare organization. All features require the user to sign in. The App is intended for users 18 years of age or older acting in a professional capacity. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account & Identity Information

  • Name, work email address, professional role/discipline (e.g., RN, PT, OT, SLP), and organization affiliation.
  • Authentication credentials and session tokens. We support email/password sign-in as well as Google and Apple sign-in; when you use those providers, we receive your name and email address from them to create and authenticate your account.

2.2 Clinical & Patient Data (PHI)

In the course of documenting home-health visits, authorized users enter and access patient health information, which may include patient names, demographics, visit notes, assessments, vitals, episodes of care, plans of care, and related clinical documentation. This information belongs to the contracting healthcare organization.

2.3 Audio Recordings

  • The App can record audio of visit notes through the device microphone when a user initiates a recording.
  • Recordings are used to generate transcriptions and AI-assisted draft documentation, then stored in association with the relevant visit.

2.4 Photographs & Documents

  • Users may capture or upload photographs (for example, wound images) using the device camera or photo library, and may attach documents, to support clinical documentation.

2.5 Location Information

  • With your permission, the App uses your device location only while the App is in use to verify check-in at the location of a scheduled visit. We do not track location in the background and do not use location for advertising.

2.6 Device & Usage Information

  • Technical information such as device type, operating system version, app version, and diagnostic/error logs used to operate, secure, and improve the App.
  • Push-notification tokens (if you enable notifications) to deliver alerts related to your work in the App.

4. How Information Is Shared

  • With your organization: Data you enter is made available to authorized users within your healthcare organization according to their roles and permissions.
  • Service providers / sub-processors: We use trusted vendors to host and operate the App (cloud hosting on Google Cloud Platform) and to provide transcription/AI processing. These providers are bound by contractual obligations, including, where applicable, HIPAA Business Associate Agreements, and may process data only on our instructions.
  • Authentication providers: Google and Apple, when you choose to sign in with those services.
  • Legal requirements: When required by law, regulation, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, and security.
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy and applicable law.

5. Data Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect information, including encryption of data in transit (TLS/HTTPS) and at rest, access controls, role-based permissions, and audit logging. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect information consistent with industry standards and applicable law.

6. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as necessary to provide the App, to comply with the contractual instructions of the healthcare organization that controls the data, and to meet legal, regulatory, and recordkeeping obligations (including medical-record retention requirements). Retention periods for PHI are determined primarily by the contracting healthcare organization.

7. Your Choices & Rights

  • Permissions: You can grant or revoke microphone, camera, photo, location, and notification permissions at any time in your device settings. Disabling a permission may limit related features.
  • Account & data requests: Because most data is controlled by your healthcare organization, requests to access, correct, or delete records should generally be directed to that organization. We will support such requests as the organization’s Business Associate.
  • Contact us using the details below for privacy questions or requests.

8. International Use

The App is operated from, and data is processed and stored in, the United States. If you access the App from outside the United States, you understand that information will be processed in the United States.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will revise the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, provide additional notice. Your continued use of the App after changes become effective constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy.
